# Roland Jupiter-8

## Variants — Important for Valuation

- **DCB vs MIDI:** Early Jupiter-8s have DCB (Roland's proprietary sync protocol) only. Later revisions added MIDI. A DCB-only unit requires a DCB-to-MIDI adapter (e.g. Kenton) to integrate with modern setups — this is a real limitation and lowers practical value. Always check whether a listing specifies MIDI or DCB.
- **12-bit vs 14-bit DACs:** Later production runs upgraded the DACs from 12-bit to 14-bit, resulting in noticeably smoother, higher-resolution audio. The 14-bit version is more desirable. The serial number range and internal inspection can confirm this — some sellers will mention it, many won't. If unspecified, assume 12-bit.
- **Voice count:** Always 8 voices, but confirm all voices are working.

## Known Issues

- **Voice card failures:** Each voice is on a separate card. Failed cards result in missing voices. Repair requires sourcing NOS or harvested parts — can be expensive and time-consuming.
- **Key contacts:** The keyboard mechanism is prone to oxidation causing dead or intermittent keys. Cleaning usually resolves it but is tedious on an 8-octave instrument.
- **Pitch bender mechanism:** A common failure point. Replacement or repair requires careful sourcing.
- **Internal battery:** For patch memory, same as other vintage Roland gear — dead batteries are a common and inexpensive fix.

## Desirable Features

- MIDI (not just DCB) — essential for practical use.
- 14-bit DACs — higher sonic quality.
- All 8 voices confirmed working.
- Original Roland case or custom flight case adds value.
- Complete with original manual and documentation.

## Red Flags

- DCB-only with no mention of MIDI adapter.
- Multiple failed voices with no repair estimate.
- "Sold as-is" on a unit at this price point is a serious warning — parts and skilled service are expensive.
- Missing or damaged pitch/mod panel or slider knobs (hard to source).

## Market Notes

- The Jupiter-8 is one of the most iconic and expensive vintage polysynths. Prices are high and relatively stable.
- DAC version and MIDI capability are the two factors most likely to be undisclosed and most impactful on value — flag when absent from a listing.
- Condition premium is steep: a non-working unit requires significant investment to restore.
