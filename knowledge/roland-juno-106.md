# Roland Juno-106

## Known Issues

- **Voice chips (80017A):** The custom Roland voice chips fail frequently — dropout, tuning instability, or silence on individual voices is almost always a failed chip. This is the Juno-106's most common fault and well-understood. Third-party replacements (Tauntek, Vecoven) are widely available at ~$20–40/chip. A listing disclosing voice chip issues is repairable and not a dealbreaker if priced accordingly — but "sold as-is" with multiple failed voices warrants a larger discount.
- **Chorus:** The chorus circuit can develop noise or crackle, commonly caused by degraded MN3009 BBD chips. Replacements are available but rarer; repair is more involved than voice chips.
- **Slider/knob oxidation:** Common on most units at this age. Usually resolves with contact cleaner and not a significant issue.

## Desirable Features

- Original Roland hard case adds meaningful value — it's large and purpose-built, hard to source separately.
- "Recently serviced" or "all 6 voices tested" is a strong positive signal.
- Some units have had capacitor replacements (recap) which is a positive sign of care.

## Red Flags

- "Sold as-is" combined with voice issues may indicate multiple failed chips (replacing all 6 is ~$200+ in parts alone).
- Missing pitch/mod bender panel is a cosmetic issue that's hard to source.
- Water damage or heavy corrosion on the PCB — not repairable at a reasonable cost.

## Market Notes

- Condition tiers matter significantly: a mint example commands a substantial premium over a fair/working example.
- Units with a documented service history or recent chip replacement sell faster and at higher prices.
