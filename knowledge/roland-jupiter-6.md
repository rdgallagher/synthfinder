# Roland Jupiter-6

## Known Issues

- **MIDI implementation:** The original MIDI on the Jupiter-6 is limited — no patch dump/load, limited CC support. This is widely considered a shortcoming and is the main reason the Europa retrofit exists.
- **Voice card failures:** Individual voice cards can fail, causing missing or out-of-tune voices. Unlike the Juno-106, the Jupiter-6 uses discrete components rather than custom chips, so repair is possible but requires skill.
- **Battery-backed RAM:** The internal battery for patch memory dies eventually. A dead battery means patches can't be saved. Inexpensive fix but worth noting if the seller hasn't addressed it.
- **Filter resonance:** Some units exhibit instability at high resonance settings — a known quirk rather than a fault.

## Desirable Features / Modifications

- **Europa MIDI retrofit:** The Europa board (by Analogue Renaissance) is the highly regarded afterthought MIDI upgrade — adds full patch save/load over MIDI, individual voice outputs, arpeggiator enhancements, and much more. A Jupiter-6 with Europa is meaningfully more useful and commands a significant premium (typically $300–500 added value). Always worth noting in listings.
- **Unison mode** and the onboard arpeggiator are features that distinguish it from the Jupiter-8.
- All voices confirmed working is a strong positive.

## Red Flags

- Missing or broken voice cards with no repair history.
- Dead patch memory (battery issue) without disclosure — suggests the seller hasn't maintained it.
- Heavy key mechanism wear or broken keys (hard to source replacements).

## Market Notes

- The Jupiter-6 sits below the Jupiter-8 in desirability but is valued for its more aggressive filter character and lighter weight.
- Europa-equipped units are a distinct tier and should be scored accordingly against other Europa units where possible.
